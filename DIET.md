# Diet Plan — Lean Muscle (eat every meal at home)

**Stats:** 6′3″, 147.2 lb, lean ex-track athlete, early 20s. Underweight (BMI ~18.4) → eat a real surplus, stay lean.

**Verified science:** total daily protein drives muscle growth. Protein timing / the "anabolic window" is a myth. There's no magic g/kg threshold — keep total protein high and consistent.

## Targets
| | |
|---|---|
| Calories | **~3,300 / day** (~400–500 over maintenance) |
| Protein | **~155 g** |
| Carbs | ~470 g |
| Fat | ~90 g |
| Water | 120 oz |

**Goal:** gain ~0.5–0.75 lb/week. Weigh weekly in the AM and adjust by the scale, not the math. If gaining >1 lb/week consistently, trim ~200 cal. Recomp worries don't matter until ~165–175 lb.

## Principles
1. The scale is the boss — set calories from your weekly weight trend.
2. Progressive overload turns the surplus into muscle, not fat.
3. Don't fear the surplus — your risk is under-eating.
4. Consistency beats perfection — tasty and repeatable wins.

- **Carbs:** white & brown rice, oats, potatoes & sweet potatoes, pasta (incl. chickpea), bagels/whole-grain bread, tortillas, quinoa

## Daily meal template (~3,300 cal)
1. **Breakfast** — egg + potato scramble (4 eggs, potatoes, cheese, spinach) + 2 toast w/ PB + glass of milk
2. **Smoothie** — the mass-gainer smoothie
3. **Lunch** — chicken-thigh burrito bowl (thighs, rice, black beans, cheese, avocado, salsa)
4. **Snack** — Greek yogurt bowl or PB protein balls + fruit
5. **Dinner** — beef & rice power bowl (85/15 beef, rice, roasted veg, olive oil) + glass of milk

## Mass-gainer smoothie (~900–1,100 cal, ~50 g protein)
1 cup whole milk + 1 cup full-fat Greek yogurt + 1 banana + ½ cup oats + 2 tbsp peanut butter + 1 scoop whey + handful spinach + 2 dates + 1 tbsp chia. Blend. Add 1 tbsp olive oil or ¼ avocado to push past 1,000 with no taste change.

## Hardgainer calorie tricks
1. Drink your calories — a smoothie or 2–3 glasses of milk = 500–1,000 cal, near-zero fullness.
2. Olive-oil drizzle on finished food = +120–240 cal, no extra volume.
3. Cook oats in milk, rice in broth — more calories per identical bite.
4. Eat calorie-dense, not voluminous — bagels over salad, rice over big veg bowls.
5. Pair every meal with a calorie drink + keep a default high-cal snack for an easy +300–500.
