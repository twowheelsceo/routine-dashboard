# 40 Habits — The System

🔬 = peer-reviewed · 🛠️ = practitioner/consensus

## The 3 laws everything hangs on
1. **If-then beats willpower** — "If [situation], then I [action]" lifts follow-through with a medium-large effect (d=.65 across 94 studies — Gollwitzer & Sheeran 2006). Highest-ROI move there is.
2. **Repetition builds automaticity** — a behavior repeated in a fixed context becomes automatic in ~66 days (range 18–254). One missed day doesn't break it — never miss twice (Lally 2010).
3. **Cues live in your environment** — ~43% of daily behavior is habit, triggered by stable surroundings (Wood 2002). Engineer the cue; don't fight the craving.

## Morning & keystone
1. **Fixed wake time, 7 days/week** 🛠️ — anchors the day's habit chain to one cue. → Alarm across the room, same time even weekends.
2. **Make your bed first** 🛠️ — a finished task in 2 min builds momentum. → Before you touch your phone.
3. **Lay out tomorrow's cues tonight** 🔬 — environment = trigger (Wood 2002). → Gym clothes by the door, scanner tab open.
4. **Write your top-3 as if-then plans** 🔬 — highest-ROI move (Gollwitzer 2006). → "If it's 9am, then I message 5 sellers."
5. **Hardest task first** 🛠️ — protects your sharpest hours. → One 60–90 min block before the noise.

## Deep work & focus
6. **Single-task in timed blocks** 🔬 — task-switching leaves attention residue (Leroy 2009). → 50–90 min, one thing.
7. **Finish or write the next step before switching** 🔬 — unfinished tasks trigger residue. → Jot the next action, then close it.
8. **Phone in another room during focus** 🔬 — removes the interruption. (The "mere presence drains your brain" study was refuted.) → In a drawer, not face-down on the desk.
9. **Notifications off + batch messages** 🔬 — pings fragment attention. → Check at the top of each hour.
10. **Time-block your calendar** 🛠️ — give every hour a job. → Plan blocks the night before.
11. **A desk used only for deep work** 🔬 — single-purpose location = cue (Wood 2002). → No scrolling or eating there.

## Discipline systems
12. **Track streaks, forgive one miss** 🔬 — one lapse is a non-event; a missed week is the risk (Lally 2010). → Never miss twice.
13. **Habit-stack onto existing anchors** 🔬 (Wood 2002). → "After I pour coffee, I write my top-3."
14. **Pre-decide meals, training & work** 🔬 — routines lower stress (Wood 2002). → Same slots weekly.
15. **Commitment device + accountability** 🛠️ — the Aidan-vs-Dante arena with real stakes.
16. **Daily non-negotiables** 🛠️ — 3–5 items that define a "won" day.

## Phone & distraction
17. **Phone charges outside the bedroom** 🛠️ — kills the night scroll and AM grab.
18. **One phone-free block daily** 🛠️ — rebuilds focus tolerance.
19. **Strip feed apps off the home screen** 🛠️ — friction kills mindless opens.
20. **Skip the "dopamine detox" hype** 🛠️ — the framing isn't well supported, but don't open the day with the feed.

## Fitness & body
21. **Train 3–5×/week on fixed days** 🛠️ — scheduled training becomes automatic.
22. **Progressive overload — log every set** 🛠️ — beat last session's numbers.
23. **Hit a high, consistent daily protein** 🔬 — total daily protein drives muscle; timing/"window" is a myth (Schoenfeld 2013). → ~155 g/day.
24. **Train on low-motivation days anyway** 🛠️ — a "minimum" session still counts.
25. **Daily movement / steps** 🛠️ — baseline health + recovery.

## Sleep & recovery
26. **Consistent sleep/wake times** 🛠️ (consensus — not verified this pass).
27. **7–9 hours, protected** 🛠️ — recovery is when muscle is built.
28. **Screens down 30–60 min before bed** 🛠️.
29. **Dark, cool room** 🛠️.

## Nutrition
30. **Protein at every meal** 🔬 (Tagawa 2021) — the trackable way to hit your total.
31. **Hydrate on a schedule** 🛠️.
32. **Eat every meal at home** 🛠️ — saves money, controls quality. Batch-cook; never default to takeout.

## Mindset & resilience
33. **10-min daily meditation** 🔬 — small-but-real gains in attention & impulse control (Zainal & Newman 2024). Not a magic fix.
34. **Evening reflection / journal** 🛠️ — what you controlled, what you'd repeat.
35. **One rep of voluntary discomfort** 🛠️ — cold shower, last set, hard call.
36. **Control dichotomy** 🛠️ — act on what's yours; ignore what isn't.

## Business & productivity
37. **Daily top-3 priorities** 🛠️ — needle-movers before busywork.
38. **Weekly review + plan** 🛠️ — Sundays, 30 min.
39. **One deliberate-practice block** 🛠️ — study the market, rehearse the pitch.
40. **Environment-design your selling** 🔬 — fixed outreach window, phone away (Wood/Leroy).

## ⚠️ Debunked popular advice (do NOT rely on these)
- **"A phone in the room drains your brain even when off"** — refuted. Removing it still helps (kills interruptions), but the "mere presence" mechanism is bunk.
- **Protein "anabolic window"** — refuted. Timing is irrelevant once daily total is matched.
- **Protein thresholds at 1.3 / 1.6 g/kg** — refuted. No hard cutoff; it's a continuum. Keep protein high and consistent.
- **Meditation as a huge impulse-control fix (g=0.643)** — refuted. Effects are real but modest.
