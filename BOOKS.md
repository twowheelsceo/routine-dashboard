# The First 10 Books — Reading List

Web-researched picks for two early-20s entrepreneurs building an AI business, getting fit, getting smarter, and mastering psychology, marketing, sales & philosophy. Read in order.

1. **Atomic Habits** — James Clear *(discipline / systems)*
   The operating system for everything else — builds the 1%-better daily systems that make fitness, learning and execution automatic.
2. **The Almanack of Naval Ravikant** — Eric Jorgenson *(wealth / thinking)*
   The mental-model bible for getting rich with leverage (code, media, equity) — wealth without trading time.
3. **Co-Intelligence** — Ethan Mollick *(AI business ★)*
   The hype-free, founder-usable guide to building with AI right now — turns "AI is coming" into a concrete playbook. **Top current AI-business pick.**
4. **$100M Offers** — Alex Hormozi *(marketing / sales)*
   Make offers so good people feel stupid saying no — pure cash-generation leverage for the flip and the AI build.
5. **Influence** — Robert Cialdini *(sales / persuasion)*
   The science of why people say yes (reciprocity, scarcity, social proof) — what every great closer steals from.
6. **Building a StoryBrand** — Donald Miller *(marketing)*
   A repeatable framework to make messaging so clear customers instantly get it — make the customer the hero.
7. **Outlive** — Peter Attia *(health / longevity)*
   The no-BS longevity manual — train and eat now so you're strong and sharp at 90, not just lean at 25.
8. **The Laws of Human Nature** — Robert Greene *(psychology)*
   A field guide to what drives people beneath what they say — read motives, manage egos, win negotiations.
9. **Thinking, Fast and Slow** — Daniel Kahneman *(smarter / thinking)*
   The Nobel map of your own biases — spot the mental traps wrecking your decisions before they cost money.
10. **Meditations** — Marcus Aurelius *(philosophy / stoicism)*
    The 2,000-year-old discipline playbook founders and athletes live by — stay calm and unshakable building hard things.
